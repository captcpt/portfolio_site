Research and Writing ProjectsProjects

LGBTQ+ People in Japan and the Impact of International Ideas

This research paper delves into the historical, social, and political aspects of LGBTQ+ experiences in Japan, employing comparative analysis and literature review. The aim is to assess, analyze, and propose logical suggestions for advancing LGBTQ+ equality in the country, particularly examining the impact of international ideas on this issue.

Policy Memo: Global Food Insecurity

This policy memo addresses global food insecurity, encompassing issues like food waste and improper storage. It highlights successful strategies, such as South Korea's approach of charging for food waste, as key identifiers for resolving these challenges. The memo further proposes solutions and examines their potential impacts on mitigating worldwide food insecurity.

Policy Memo: Affordable Care Act

The Affordable Care Act (ACA) was introduced in response to widespread uninsured individuals in the 21st-century United States. While it expanded health insurance access and reduced disparities, challenges remain in program implementation and private insurance coverage limitations, contributing to ongoing controversy. Despite its effectiveness within the American political landscape, there are recommendations for additional policies to address high healthcare costs.

Lung Cancer in Hungary

Lung cancer has emerged as a leading cause of mortality globally, and Hungary is experiencing a notable increase in cancer incidence, ranking fourth worldwide. This epidemiological overview focuses on the prevalence of lung cancer in Hungary, shedding light on the significant impact of this non-communicable disease in the country.

Tragedy of the Commons

Expanding upon traditional game theory models for the Tragedy of the Commons, this project aims to reconstruct and extend the framework to offer a more nuanced understanding of collective resource management. By incorporating additional variables and behavioral factors, the enhanced model seeks to provide a comprehensive analysis of how agents interact within shared resource systems. The goal is to contribute insights that go beyond the classical paradigm, offering a more realistic representation of decision-making dynamics in addressing the challenges posed by the Tragedy of the Commons.

Intelligence Estimate

This paper utilizes diverse raw data sources, including historical records, mapping, and phone taps, to construct an intelligence estimate and strategic plan for the fictional countries of Arcadia and Bandria. The focus is on attributing the June 11, 2021 attacks in Arcadia to the Peace for Greywall (PGM) movement, examining potential collaboration with Bandrian and Arcadian supporters and anticipating future developments in the Greywall independence movement.
